The real cost

Where agencies bleed money.

Every one of these is a build we've done. Every one was costing the agency real, countable revenue every single month.

📈

Leads go cold before anyone follows up

An inbound lead hits your inbox. Someone means to reply. It's been 48 hours. They've already signed with someone else. At $5K–$20K per client, that's not a small leak. For a 10-client agency, one missed lead per month is $60K–$240K/year walking out the door.

🔁

Onboarding is the same manual slog every time

New client signed. Someone spends 2–3 hours sending contracts, setting up folders, writing the welcome email, creating logins. Times twelve clients a year. That's weeks of your team's time. At $75/hr fully-loaded, that's $2,700–$4,050 of salaried time spent on copy-paste work every year.

📊

Reporting takes a half-day to produce

Your client wants a report. Someone pulls data from four tools, pastes it into a deck, formats it manually. Every week or every month. It's your most expensive admin task and it adds zero revenue. 4 hours/month × 10 clients = 40 hours of senior time gone. That's a full work week, every single month.

🧩

Your tools don't talk to each other

HubSpot, Monday, Slack, QuickBooks, your proposal tool — all siloed. Data gets re-entered. Things fall through the gaps. Your ops run on copy-paste and tribal knowledge. Manual data re-entry alone typically costs agencies 5–8 hours/week in staff time. At $75/hr, that's $19,500–$31,200/year in pure waste.

👤

Clients have no visibility — so they email constantly

"Just checking in on the status..." You know this message. It comes because clients have no live window into their account. So they ask. And your team stops to answer. Every. Single. Time. 10 clients × 3 interruptions/week = 30 context switches. Context-switching costs ~23 minutes of focus per incident. That's 11.5 hours of deep work evaporated weekly.

💸

You know what needs building — but can't get it done

The ideas exist. The backlog exists. The agency quote came back at $25,000 and six weeks. So nothing gets built, the manual workarounds stay, and the leaks keep running. Every month you don't fix your ops is another month of compounding inefficiency. That backlog isn't a to-do list — it's a liability with a monthly interest rate.
