Clearspace rule

Always maintain a minimum clearspace equal to the cap-height of the "D" on all four sides. Never crowd or alter the weight or colour of either word. "Done" is always roman, "Fast" is always italic lime.

Colour rules
Lime is the only accent colour. It's used for the logo wordmark, primary CTAs, interactive highlights, and status indicators. Use it sparingly — it only hits hard when surrounded by dark.
The black background is non-negotiable. It's what makes the lime pop and gives the brand its premium, technical edge. Never swap it for grey or navy.
Never use lime as a background for large sections. It works on CTAs and small highlights only. Over-use kills the contrast that makes it work.
Don't add new colours. If a design problem needs a colour not in this palette, solve it with opacity or surface layering instead.

06 — Voice & Tone

How we talk.

"We're the operator's operator. We don't sell software — we solve the problem and move on to the next one."

DoneFast is a peer, not a vendor. We speak plainly, we move fast, and we let results do the bragging. The tone is direct and confident — never corporate, never breathless, never hype-y. We don't use jargon because the people we work with are too busy for it.

Write like this
"We build it. You approve it. Done." — Short, declarative, active voice.
"Most automations ship in 1–3 days." — Specific, honest, no waffle.
"That's not an ops problem. That's a compounding revenue problem." — Reframe with authority.
"Submit as many requests as you want." — Generous, confident, no caveats up front.
"We'd rather earn your business every month than lock you in." — Trust-building without begging.
Not like this
"Leverage our cutting-edge AI-powered solution ecosystem." — Meaningless filler.
"We're passionate about helping agencies thrive!" — Hollow enthusiasm.
"Our best-in-class platform..." — Nobody believes this anymore.
"Please don't hesitate to reach out!" — Weak, desperate energy.
"We work with businesses of all sizes across many verticals." — Too broad, says nothing.
Confident
We don't hedge. We state things plainly and back them with specifics. "7-day refund" not "we'll try to make it right."
Specific
Numbers over adjectives. "1–3 business days" beats "fast turnaround." "10–20 hours saved per week" beats "significant time savings."
Human
We write like a smart peer who respects the reader's time. Contractions are fine. Short sentences are better than long ones.
