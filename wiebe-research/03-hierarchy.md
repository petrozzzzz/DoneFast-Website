## Section 1: Hero

**Job:** In 5 seconds, communicate: (1) what this is — a done-for-you AI implementation service, (2) who it's for — operators who know they need AI but don't know what to do, (3) why care — flat fee, fast, no agency BS.

**Dominant message:** "You know you need AI. You don't know what to build, what to use, or who to trust. That's what we're for."

**Anchor quotes from research:**
- "the biggest challenge isn't adopting AI, it's knowing where to start without feeling overwhelmed" — oneclickcontractor
- "Most small business owners feel stuck at square one, overwhelmed by options" — sketchdev
- Pete's discovery quote: "every business owner knows they need AI in their business — but they haven't spent the last few years living inside it like I have"
- "promises made by many AI automation agencies greatly oversell" — aiifi (rejected — too negative for hero, save for section 3)

**Headline candidates (ranked):**

1. **"You know you need AI. You don't know who to trust."** — strongest. Mirrors buyer's internal monologue. Three of three on the 5-second test (subhead carries "what is it").
2. "AI in your business. Without the agency runaround." — solid, less empathetic, more confrontational.
3. "Built by an AI nerd. Priced like an honest one." — leans on Pete's voice, fun but loses non-tech buyer.
4. "Most AI agencies are running a scam. We're the alternative." — too aggressive for hero; deploy in section 3.

**Recommendation:** **#1.** Use a softer, more empathetic hook for cold traffic; save the aggressive frame for after the buyer's curiosity is engaged.

**Eyebrow:** "For business owners tired of $5,000 quotes for a Zapier flow."
*(Source: aiifi pain quotes + the existing live page eyebrow which already tested at this awareness level.)*

**Subheadline candidates:**
1. **"Custom AI agents, automations, and internal tools — built and managed for $1,250/month flat. We tell you what to build first. We ship it in 3-5 days. You get refunded 75% if you don't love it."** — long but loaded. Three risk-reversals.
2. "We're the productized AI service for non-tech operators. Flat fee. Real engineering. No deck."
3. "$1,250/month. We figure out what to build. We ship it in days, not months. 75% back if it's not for you."

**Recommendation:** **#3** — punchier, leads with price, all three risk-reversals visible.

**Primary CTA:** "Book a 15-min call"
**Trust strip:** "$1,250/mo flat · 75% refund first week · 3-5 day delivery · Cancel anytime"

## Section 3: The category critique (big-idea moment)

**Job:** Validate the buyer's existing belief that AI agencies are mostly scams. Position DoneFast as the deliberate alternative. This is where we earn the right to charge $1,250 and ask for a call.

**Dominant message:** "Yes, most AI agencies are bad. Here's exactly how, and here's how we're different."

**Anchor quotes from research:**
- "Reports, slide decks, roadmaps. Nice looking documents that make everyone feel like progress happened. But you didn't hire someone for documents." — danielbilsborough
- "promises made by many AI automation agencies greatly oversell current real-world AI capabilities" — aiifi
- "hook desperate small businesses with cheap initial offers then trap them in expensive long-term contracts" — downelink
- "An AI consultant who hasn't shipped anything in six months is selling you history." — danielbilsborough
- "60-page strategy doc is outdated before the ink dries" — danielbilsborough
- Gartner: "90% of AI agent vendors are scams"

**Headline candidates:**
1. **"Most AI agencies are running a scam."** — short, declarative, weaponizes the buyer's existing belief.
2. "The AI agency you're about to hire is going to send you a deck."
3. "AI consulting is a $250 billion industry. Most of it is fluff."

**Recommendation:** **#1**, with the qualifier in the body that we're not name-calling — we're describing a documented pattern.

**Body draft (compose from quotes):**
> Most agencies in this space sell you decks. Strategy reviews. Sixty-page roadmaps that are outdated before the ink dries. They quote $5,000 to $15,000 to build a single Zapier flow. They lock you into 6-month contracts. They learn AI on your dime.
>
> Gartner says 90% of AI agent vendors are scams. We don't disagree.
>
> We're not them. We ship working AI — agents, automations, internal tools — for $1,250/month flat. No deck. No strategy review. No contract. If we don't earn it in week one, we refund 75%.

## Section 11: FAQ / objection handling

**Job:** Neutralize the six recurring objections from research. Buyer voice in question, direct answer in body.

**6 objections (from the objection map in research, section 5):**

1. **"Aren't all of these AI agencies scams?"**
   Most are. We agree with you. The 75% refund after week one exists because we know you've been burned and we'd rather lose 25% of one month's fee than have you stuck with us.

2. **"Will this just be ChatGPT in a wrapper?"**
   AI builds the first version. A senior engineer tests, reviews, and rewrites whatever's wrong before you see it. You never get untested code. The AI is a force multiplier, not a replacement for engineering judgment.

3. **"What if I get locked in?"**
   You can't. Month-to-month. Pause anytime, your remaining days roll over. Cancel anytime, no exit fee, no penalty. You own everything we built from day one.

4. **"I don't even know what to ask you to build."**
   That's what the first call is for. Bring the boring problem that's eating your week. We'll tell you the one or two builds that move the needle most. You walk away with a plan whether you sign up or not.

5. **"What if AI moves so fast that what you build is obsolete in three months?"**
   Then we ship the new version next month. We work in 3-5 day cycles, not 6-month projects. The whole model is built around the speed of change.

6. **"Is this guy actually qualified?"**
   I've been building with AI since before there was a market for it. The world's just catching up now. I started DoneFast because I watched too many business owners get charged $7,500 for a Zapier flow by people who learned about AI three months ago.
   *— Pete, founder*

## Cross-section notes

### Visual hierarchy
- Hero gets the most weight. 70-80vh, big type.
- Section 3 (category critique) and Section 8 (why $1,250) need contrast — lean into the differentiation visually (different background, sharper edges).
- Pricing card needs to be the most visually arresting object on the page.
- Founder note is small, intentionally human-feeling.

### Section-to-section flow
1. Hero hooks with empathy.
2. Logos build a hint of credibility.
3. Category critique earns the buyer's skepticism by validating it.
4. Empathy section deepens the emotional buy-in.
5. Solution overview gives the relief — "ah, here's what they actually do."
6. How it works grounds the abstract.
7. What we build makes it real with examples.
8. Why $1,250 anchors the price as a steal.
9. Pricing closes.
10. Founder note adds a face.
11. FAQ catches stragglers.
12. Final CTA closes.

### Sections deliberately omitted
- **Testimonial / case study section** — no clients yet. The 75% guarantee + founder note + "worked-with" logos do the trust work instead. Add this section in v2 once 5+ clients exist.
- **Standalone benefits 6-card grid** — folded into solution overview to avoid repetition.
- **"Unlimited explained" full section** — moved to FAQ; defensive content shouldn't take up a full section.
- **Ticker / scrolling tag list** — provides minimal value, can be retained as decoration but no copy effort spent on it.

### CTA consistency
All CTAs point to the same Cal.com link (`donefast/15min`). Button text varies by location:
- Nav: "Book a call"

- Section 7/8 inline: "Show me what we'd build"
- Pricing: "Book a 15-min call"
- FAQ: "Book a call"
